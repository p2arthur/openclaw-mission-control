This skill guides interview preparation: answering behavioral questions effectively, drilling technical problems, and building confidence before interviews.

Interviews are about demonstrating competence AND cultural fit. Good prep means you know your stories, understand the company, and stay calm.

## STAR Framework

STAR is the gold standard for answering behavioral questions. It transforms vague stories into structured, compelling narratives.

**STAR = Situation, Task, Action, Result**

### Situation
Set the scene. Give just enough context so the interviewer understands the complexity.
- What was the project or problem?
- When did this happen?
- Who was involved?
- What was the landscape?

(30 seconds max)

### Task
What was YOUR responsibility? What did you own?
- Your specific role on the team
- What were you accountable for?
- What constraint or challenge did you face?

(15-30 seconds)

### Action
What did YOU do? Be specific and personal.
- Don't say "the team"; say "I"
- Walk through the sequence of decisions/actions
- Highlight your specific contribution
- Emphasize ownership and leadership

(60-90 seconds — this is the meat)

### Result
What happened? How do you measure success?
- Outcome of your action
- Metric if possible (time, money, customer satisfaction, team impact)
- What did you learn?
- How did you grow?

(30-60 seconds)

## Common Behavioral Questions

Prepare STAR stories for these common questions:

### "Tell me about a time you..."

**...failed and what you learned**
- Story: Project that didn't work, why, what happened
- Your learning: What changed in how you approach problems?
- Outcome: How did you apply it next time?

**...overcame a difficult challenge**
- The challenge: What made it hard?
- Your approach: How did you break it down?
- Result: What did you solve? How?

**...disagreed with someone and how you handled it**
- Situation: What was the disagreement?
- Your perspective: Why did you disagree?
- Resolution: How did you navigate it? What was the outcome?
- Learning: What did you understand about their perspective?

**...prioritized when you had too much work**
- Problem: Multiple competing priorities, limited time
- Your approach: How did you decide? What criteria?
- Result: What got done? What got deferred? Why was that okay?

**...led or influenced without authority**
- Situation: What needed to happen? You weren't in charge.
- Your approach: How did you convince/motivate others?
- Result: Did it work? What did you learn about influence?

**...worked with a difficult person**
- Situation: Who was difficult? Why?
- Your approach: How did you adapt or find common ground?
- Outcome: Did the working relationship improve? Did the project succeed?

**...took initiative**
- Situation: What was missing or broken?
- Your action: What did you do without being asked?
- Result: How did it help? What impact did it have?

## Company Research

Before every interview:

**Company Landscape:**
- What problem do they solve?
- Who are their customers?
- How do they make money?
- What stage are they (early-stage, scaling, mature)?

**Culture & Values:**
- What do they say about their culture?
- What's the org structure?
- Who are the leadership team?
- What's their growth strategy?

**Role Specifics:**
- What does this role actually do?
- Who do they report to?
- What team are they joining?
- What are the pain points they're trying to solve?

**Interview Preparation:**
- Write down 2-3 genuine questions about the role/company
- Research the interviewer (LinkedIn, company blog) if their name is known
- Have an example ready of something you admire about the company or product

## Technical Interview Strategy

For technical interviews (coding problems, case studies, design):

### Strategy Before the Interview
- Identify the skill they're testing (data structures? system design? problem-solving?)
- Practice 5-10 problems of similar difficulty
- Don't memorize solutions; understand patterns
- Practice explaining your thinking out loud

### Strategy During the Interview

**Clarify before coding**
- Ask questions about the problem (edge cases? constraints? scale?)
- Explain your approach before writing code
- State your assumptions

**Communicate while coding**
- Narrate your thinking: "I'm using a hash map because..."
- Walk through examples as you code
- Check with the interviewer: "Does this approach make sense?"

**Test and iterate**
- Test with examples (happy path + edge cases)
- Walk through the code with the interviewer
- Discuss optimization: "This is O(n²) time. We could optimize to O(n log n) if we..."

**Practice problems**
- LeetCode (easiest → medium, medium → hard)
- Company-specific: check what problems others have reported
- System design: Grok System Design, DesignGurus YouTube

## Managing Interview Anxiety

Job interviews feel high-stakes. That's normal. Here's how to manage it:

### Before the Interview
- **Reframe:** This is a conversation where you show what you know. Not a test where you fail.
- **Practice:** Do a mock interview with someone. Get feedback. Do it again.
- **Prepare:** Review your STAR stories. Research the company. Know your examples.
- **Sleep:** You think better rested. No cramming the night before.
- **Deep breaths:** 5 minutes before → 4-count in, 6-count out. Activates your calm nervous system.

### During the Interview
- **Pause:** Don't panic if you don't know something. Say "let me think about that for a second."
- **Speak up:** If you didn't understand a question, ask for clarification.
- **Admit gaps:** "I haven't worked with that tool, but I'm strong with X and I learn quickly."
- **Show your thinking:** Interviewers care more about HOW you think than the right answer.

### After the Interview
- **No more changes:** Can't un-ring the bell. Move on to the next interview.
- **Reflect:** What went well? What would you do differently? (Might help in next rounds.)
- **Follow up:** Send a brief thank-you note within 24 hours.

## Interview Red Flags

Pay attention to how the company treats you:
- Do they run on time or keep you waiting?
- Do they ask thoughtful questions or just go through a checklist?
- Do they answer your questions or deflect?
- Do you feel respected or talked down to?

The interview is also YOUR chance to evaluate them. If something feels off, trust that feeling.

## Iteration & Feedback

After each interview, log what happened:
- What questions did they ask?
- How did your STAR answers land?
- What did they dig into? (Sometimes a deep dive means high interest.)
- What would you do differently?

Use this feedback to iterate your stories and prep for the next round.
