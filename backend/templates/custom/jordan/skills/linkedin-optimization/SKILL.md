This skill guides LinkedIn optimization: headline, summary, visibility, and strategy for attracting recruiter outreach and warm connections.

LinkedIn is a visibility tool. A strong profile gets you inbound interest; a weak profile limits your reach.

## LinkedIn Profile Audit

Your profile has 3-5 seconds to hook someone. Here's what matters:

### Photo
- Professional headshot (not a selfie, not a group photo)
- Good lighting, plain background
- Genuine smile; you want to look approachable
- Your face should take up ~60% of the frame
- Consistent with how you look IRL (people expect to recognize you at an interview)

### Headline
**Current format (auto-generated):**
> Current title at Company Name | Previous titles

**Better format (custom):**
Pick one of these templates, fill in your strengths:

**Template 1 — Problem-solver focus:**
> Senior Backend Engineer | Scaling distributed systems | API architecture | Postgres optimization

**Template 2 — Skill focus:**
> Full-Stack Engineer | React + Node.js | Real-time systems | Team lead

**Template 3 — Impact focus:**
> Product Engineer | Built features for 1M+ users | Reduced latency 40% | Mentoring 2 junior engineers

**Template 4 — Role + open signal:**
> Staff Engineer at Google | Open to: Senior/Staff roles | Distributed systems | Mentorship

What matters:
- Lead with your role/level
- 3-5 keywords that match your target job descriptions
- Could include: "open to opportunities" if that signals well to you
- Avoid: Motivational quotes, emojis, vague language

### About Section
**Bad (generic):**
> Passionate about technology and building great products. Love working with talented teams. Always learning.

**Good (specific and confident):**
> I'm a backend engineer who loves building scalable systems. Most of my work has been on distributed databases and API design — I've led 3 major migrations that each improved performance by 30%+.

> Right now I'm interested in roles where I can:
> - Own a technical area end-to-end (not just task work)
> - Work with teams that care about code quality
> - Learn from engineers stronger than me

> If you're hiring for senior backend roles on Postgres/Go systems, let's chat.

What matters:
- Specific about what you've done (not vague)
- Clear about what you want (roles, companies, problems)
- Conversational tone (not corporate)
- 3-4 short paragraphs (not a novel)

### Experience Section
- Keep it accurate and detailed (same as resume, or slightly longer)
- 3-5 bullet points per role showing impact
- Use keywords that match target roles

### Skills & Endorsements
- List 10-15 skills (top skills that matter for target roles)
- Don't hide irrelevant skills; just deprioritize them
- Remove endorsements for skills you don't want associated with you
- Order by relevance (move target skills to top)

### Recommendations
- Ask managers/colleagues for LinkedIn recommendations (especially if you're interviewing)
- Write recommendations for others first (increases likelihood they'll reciprocate)
- Aim for 3-5 recommendations on your profile

## Keyword Optimization

LinkedIn search works like any other search: keywords matter.

**Strategy:**
1. Pick your target job description
2. Extract 20-30 keywords (skills, tools, concepts)
3. Audit your profile: do these keywords appear?
4. Add them naturally in headline, summary, experience bullets

**Example:**
Target role wants: "React, Next.js, TypeScript, GraphQL, performance optimization, mentoring"

Your profile should mention:
- Headline: React, Next.js, performance optimization
- Summary: typescript, GraphQL, mentoring
- Experience bullets: react, optimization, typescript, mentoring

Recruiters search for these keywords. Your profile shows up → you get inbound.

## Connection Strategy

LinkedIn connections are your network. Strategy matters.

### Tier 1: Warm Connections
- People you actually know (former colleagues, classmates, friends)
- Add with a note: "Hey [name], great to reconnect. Let's catch up soon."
- Why: They're real relationships, higher engagement, referrals

### Tier 2: Target Company Employees
- People working at companies on your target list
- Research them first (hiring manager? Engineer? People you want to learn from?)
- Add with a note: "Hi [name], I'm interested in [company] for [role] and have always admired your work on [specific thing]. Would love to chat about culture/opportunities."
- Why: Warm path into target companies

### Tier 3: Recruiters & Industry Peers
- Recruiters in your space (many will connect proactively)
- Peers in your field (for learning, industry connection)
- Add with a note: "Hi [name], always impressed by your work on [thing]. Would love to connect."
- Why: Visibility, potential opportunities, industry connection

### Avoid:
- Spamming random people with "Let's connect" (low signal)
- Accepting every recruiter connection (some are scams; vet them)
- Being someone who connects then never engages (bad signal)

## Visibility & Engagement

Just having a profile isn't enough. Be discoverable.

### Content to Post
- **Thoughts on trends:** "X is the future of backend architecture because..."
- **Lessons learned:** "Here's what I learned shipping feature X..."
- **Advice:** "5 things I wish I knew about distributed systems:"
- **Celebrations:** New role, milestone, project launch
- **Questions:** "How do you handle Y problem?" (Starts conversations)

**Frequency:** 2-4 posts per month (not daily; not never)

### Engagement
- Comment on posts from people in your network
- Like posts from target companies or people you admire
- Reply to recruiter messages (even if not interested, be professional)

## Recruiter Outreach Management

Recruiters will come. Some are good, some are noise.

### Evaluating Recruiter Messages
**Good recruiter signals:**
- Specific about the role (not "we have roles")
- Mentions specific skills from your profile
- Company name and details
- Asks a real question ("What's important to you in your next role?")

**Red flag signals:**
- Generic spam ("We're hiring!")
- No company name ("A growth-stage startup")
- Pressure tactics ("Rare opportunity, limited time")
- Asking for your password or sensitive info

### Responding to Recruiters
**If interested:**
> "Thanks for reaching out. I'm interested in [role] at [company]. Here's what I'm looking for: [requirements]. Can you tell me more about the team and growth trajectory?"

**If not interested:**
> "Thanks for thinking of me. [Company] isn't in my target list right now, but I'd be happy to help if you have other opportunities that fit [your targets]."

**If you want to stay on their radar:**
> "Not actively looking right now, but I'm open to the right opportunity. Let's stay connected and chat in [timeframe]."

## Profile Maintenance

Update your profile regularly:
- After a promotion or new role
- After completing a significant project
- When your target changes
- Quarterly (even if just a small tweak)
