This skill guides resume optimization: turning job descriptions into achievement bullets, formatting for ATS parsing, and tailoring for specific roles.

A good resume bullet tells a story: problem → action → result, with a metric that proves it worked.

## Resume Bullet Formula

**Bad:**
> Responsible for building features and managing projects.

**Good:**
> Led migration of customer authentication system to OAuth, reducing login failures by 40% and cutting security incident response time from 4 hours to 15 minutes.

**Formula:** [Action] [project/initiative], [impact/benefit], [metric/proof].

Breaking down:
- **Action:** What did you do? (Led, built, architected, designed, reduced, improved, grew)
- **Project:** What was the thing? (Product feature, system, process, team initiative)
- **Impact:** Why did it matter? (User experience, speed, reliability, revenue, team capacity)
- **Metric:** What's the evidence? (Percentage, time saved, money made/saved, scale)

### Strong Verbs
Avoid: responsible for, helped with, involved in, worked on
Use: Led, architected, optimized, reduced, grew, improved, migrated, designed, shipped, prevented, diagnosed

### Quantify Everything
- Don't say: "Improved performance"
- Say: "Optimized database queries, reducing API response time from 800ms to 200ms"

- Don't say: "Expanded reach"
- Say: "Grew user base from 10k to 100k through SEO optimization and referral program"

- Don't say: "Enhanced reliability"
- Say: "Reduced system downtime by 99% through automated monitoring and failover"

## Tailoring for Job Descriptions

When applying to a specific role:

1. **Extract keywords:** Read the JD, find the top 5-7 skills/outcomes they value
2. **Match your bullets:** Do you have experience that demonstrates each skill?
3. **Reorder your bullets:** Put the most relevant bullet first, even if it's not your "biggest"
4. **Update language:** Use their language where it aligns with your experience

**Example:**

JD emphasizes:
> "Expert in React/Next.js, strong TypeScript skills, experience with performance optimization and UI component libraries"

Your resume should lead with:
> Led React/Next.js component library migration for 50+ internal teams, reducing bundle size by 35% through tree-shaking optimization and improving developer velocity by ~2 hours per sprint.

Instead of:
> Worked on various frontend projects using React.

## ATS Optimization

ATS (Applicant Tracking Systems) parse resumes algorithmically. Make it easy:

### Format for Parsing
- **Simple structure:** Use standard sections (Summary, Experience, Skills, Education)
- **One column layout:** Multi-column PDFs confuse parsers; single-column works best
- **Standard fonts:** Avoid decorative fonts, icons, graphics
- **No tables or boxes:** Parsers skip them
- **Standard section headers:** "Experience", "Skills", "Education" (not "Wins" or "Cool Stuff")

### Keywords Matter
ATS matches your resume against job description keywords:
- If JD says "React" and your resume says "React", match ✓
- If JD says "React" and your resume only says "frontend", weak match ✗

**Keyword strategy:**
1. Read the JD
2. Extract 20-30 keywords (skills, tools, concepts)
3. Audit your resume: do these keywords appear?
4. Add them naturally in experience bullets and skills section

### Skills Section
List technical skills, tools, and frameworks. This is keyword real estate:
```
Skills: React, Next.js, TypeScript, Node.js, PostgreSQL, AWS (EC2, RDS, Lambda),
Docker, Git, REST APIs, GraphQL, CSS/Tailwind, Jest, Git, CICD (GitHub Actions)
```

Don't write prose; just list what you know.

## Structure

**Order:** Summary/Objective (optional) → Experience → Skills → Education → Certifications

**Experience section:**
- Company name, title, dates (month/year)
- 3-5 bullets per role (focus on recent, relevant roles; less detail for older ones)
- Format: verb + action + metric

**Skills section:**
- Group by category if helpful: "Languages: Python, JavaScript, Go. Databases: PostgreSQL, Redis. Tools: Docker, Kubernetes"
- Or just list alphabetically: "React, TypeScript, Node.js, PostgreSQL, AWS..."

**Education:**
- Degree, school, year (GPA optional unless > 3.8)

**Certifications:**
- Include if relevant to target role; skip otherwise

## Length

**1 page:** Standard for early-career (< 5 years)
**2 pages:** Acceptable for mid-career (5-10 years) or highly relevant deep experience
**3+ pages:** Rarely necessary; likely means you're not prioritizing what matters

Don't artificially pad or compress. Aim for clear, dense bullets with high signal-to-noise.

## Iterating Based on Feedback

If you're not getting interviews:
- **Problem 1:** Resume doesn't show relevance to target role
  - Solution: Audit job descriptions you're applying to; increase keyword match
  - Reorder bullets to lead with most relevant experience

- **Problem 2:** Bullets lack metrics or proof
  - Solution: Add numbers (time, percentage, scale) to every bullet
  - Replace vague language with specific outcomes

- **Problem 3:** ATS not parsing
  - Solution: Simplify format; remove graphics, tables, decorative fonts
  - Use standard section headers; put phone/email near top

- **Problem 4:** Getting interviews but not advancing
  - Problem is likely not the resume (it passed the screener)
  - Focus on interview prep, not resume optimization
