This skill guides job search strategy: defining targets, managing pipeline velocity, tracking conversions, and knowing when to pivot.

A job search is a project. Treat it with strategy and metrics, not hope and spray-and-pray applications.

## Target Definition

Before applying anywhere, get clear on what you're looking for:

### Role Definition
**Ideal role:**
- Title(s) you're pursuing (Senior Engineer? Staff Engineer? Tech Lead?)
- Focus areas (backend, full-stack, team lead?)
- Type of work (greenfield? scaling existing? legacy modernization?)

**Must-haves (deal-breakers):**
- What's non-negotiable? (Remote option? No on-call? Specific industry?)
- What would make you walk away from an offer?

**Nice-to-haves:**
- What would make the role feel amazing? (Specific tech stack? Team size? Growth trajectory?)

### Company Definition
**Stage:**
- Startup (chaos, equity, high risk/reward)? Series A/B (growth, established product)? Scale-up (processes, structure, less chaos)? Mature (stability, resources, less cutting-edge)?
- What stage do you want? What have you done before? What excites you?

**Size:**
- Early-stage (engineering as a function): <30 people
- Growth (multiple teams): 30-200 people
- Scale (departments, org structure): 200-5000 people
- Mature (enterprise): 5000+ people
- What size plays to your strengths?

**Culture & Values:**
- How do they describe culture? (Data-driven? Move fast? Long-term thinking?)
- Does that align with how you work?

**Industry/Domain:**
- Do you care? (Many strong engineers don't.) If yes, which domains interest you?
- Avoid: industries you actively dislike

**Location/Remote:**
- Full remote? Office required? Hybrid?
- If location matters, which cities/countries?

### Market Research
**Salary ranges:**
- Levels.fyi (crowdsourced, searchable by company/role/location)
- Blind (anonymous, tech worker discussions)
- Glassdoor (public, varying reliability)
- Ask people in your network who work at target companies

**Company viability:**
- Funding status: Crunchbase, AngelList, PitchBook (is the company well-funded?)
- Growth trajectory: Check recent news, hiring volume (are they growing or shrinking?)
- Culture signals: Glassdoor reviews (take with salt), Blind posts, company social media

## Target List Development

Build a list of 15-20 target companies. Prioritize into tiers:

### Tier 1: Warm Targets
- You know someone there (warm referral) OR
- They're actively hiring for your role AND
- They're growing/stable

**Why:** Referrals convert at 20-30%. Companies hiring actively move faster.

### Tier 2: Cool Targets
- You're genuinely interested AND
- They're at a stage/size you want AND
- They're hiring

**Why:** These are where you'll spend most applications. Good conversion if your resume resonates.

### Tier 3: Reach Targets
- High-prestige OR highly specific role match AND
- You're slightly under-qualified but could do it

**Why:** Lower conversion, but worth a few shots if you're interested.

**Avoid:** Tier 4 (spray-and-pray applications to random companies). They're low signal, low conversion, and emotionally draining.

## Application Pipeline Management

You're running a funnel. Track it like a sales pipeline.

### Metrics to Track
- **Applications submitted:** Count per week
- **Response rate:** How many applications → first-round interview? (30% is good; 10-20% is normal)
- **Interview-to-offer rate:** How many interviews → offer? (Varies wildly, but 20-50% is reasonable)
- **Time per phase:** Application to first-round? First-round to offer? (3-6 weeks typical)

### Application Velocity
**Target:** 5-10 high-quality applications per week (adjust based on role specificity)

**High-quality = personalized:**
- You've researched the company (not a generic application)
- Your resume is tailored to the role
- If there's a cover letter, it's personalized (not boilerplate)
- You've leveraged a warm referral if possible

**Low-quality (avoid):**
- Generic applications to 100 companies
- No customization, no referral
- Spray-and-pray wastes your time and depresses you when you get no responses

### Conversion Rate Analysis

Track which sources convert best:
- Warm referrals vs cold applications
- Which companies move fast vs slow?
- Which job boards (LinkedIn, built.in, AngelList) send qualified companies?

**Use this data:** Double down on warm referrals. Prioritize companies with fast-moving pipelines. Deprioritize or skip companies with glacial hiring.

## Interview Feedback Loop

After each interview (passed or failed), document:
- **Question:** What did they ask?
- **Your answer:** How did you respond?
- **Feedback:** What did they say? (Direct feedback, or reading between the lines?)
- **Pattern:** Is this the 2nd time they asked this? Do you need to prep differently?

### Common Interview Feedback Patterns
- **"Very strong technical skills, but concerned about communication"** → Practice explaining your thinking out loud. Do mock interviews.
- **"Love your background, but we need someone with [specific skill]"** → Do you want to learn it? If yes, mention you're learning it. If no, that company might not be the fit.
- **"Great culture fit, but need more experience with [thing]"** → Either you have a gap (address it) or they're gatekeeping. If many companies say it, address it. If one company, might be them being overly rigid.

## Strategy Pivots

If applications aren't getting interviews:

**Problem 1: Resume not converting**
- Check keywords: Does your resume match the job description language?
- Check metrics: Do your bullets show impact with numbers?
- Check ATS parsing: Is your format friendly to parsing?
- Solution: Revise resume, reorder bullets to match role, resubmit to same targets.

**Problem 2: Not enough warm referrals**
- You're relying on cold applications
- Solution: LinkedIn outreach, informational interviews, network asks
- Check: Have you asked your network for referrals? Explicitly?

**Problem 3: Target list is wrong**
- You're applying but not getting interviews
- Could mean: Target role is too niche, targets are overqualified for you, wrong experience signal
- Solution: Adjust targets. Add more "Tier 2" companies. Less focus on "Tier 3 reach" if not converting. Reconsider role title (maybe you're "Staff Engineer" not "Senior Principal"?).

**Problem 4: Interview conversion is low**
- Getting interviews but no offers
- This is an interview prep problem, not a target list problem
- Solution: Focus on interview skill (STAR prep, mock interviews, technical practice)

**Problem 5: Job search is taking longer than expected**
- You thought you'd have an offer in 4 weeks, it's been 8
- Reasons: Wrong targets, slow-moving companies, interview feedback loops, personal limits
- Solution: Adjust timeline expectations, keep momentum, keep applying even while in interview processes

## Application Cadence

**Sustainable rhythm:**
- Monday-Wednesday: Apply to 2-3 companies
- Thursday-Friday: Interview prep/follow-ups/outreach for warm referrals
- Weekends: Decompress (job search is exhausting)

**Avoid:** 20 applications one week, 0 the next. Consistency > intensity.

## Leverage Warm Referrals

Warm referrals convert 5-10x better than cold applications:

**Steps:**
1. Identify warm connection (someone who knows someone at the company)
2. Ask if they'd be willing to refer you
3. They submit your name through the referral system
4. Apply formally (important: you still need to apply; referral speeds up review)
5. Follow up: "I was referred by [name], applying for [role], happy to chat if you have questions"

**Referral language:**
- Don't: "Can you refer me?" (too much pressure)
- Do: "I'm interested in [company]. You worked there — would you feel comfortable referring me? (Only if you genuinely think we're a good fit; no pressure.)"

## When to Pivot or Escalate

**Escalate after 2-3 weeks of no responses:**
- Check targets and application quality
- Adjust resume or targets
- Increase warm outreach

**Pivot entirely if:**
- 6+ weeks, <5 interviews scheduled
- Interview feedback suggests you're in the wrong role/market
- Personal constraints have changed (timeline, salary needs, location)
- You're burning out; job search is supposed to be a marathon, not a sprint

_Strategy + consistency + adaptation = job offers. Trust the process. 💼_
